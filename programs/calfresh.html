CalFresh

Get money to help put food on the table

How does CalFresh work?

If you are approved for CalFresh (formerly known as Food Stamps or SNAP), you'll receive an EBT card that looks like a debit card. Your card will be automatically reloaded every month.

You can use your EBT card at most food stores and farmer's markets to buy food. Benefits may not be used for items such as alcoholic beverages, cigarettes, or paper products.

How do I apply for CalFresh?


  1. Fill out an application online or mail one in


  2. Attend an interview with someone from CalFresh in-person or over the phone

    You'll need to bring or send some documents, potentially including identification, proof of residency, immigration papers, housing/utility/medical bills, or income statements.


  3. Start receiving money on your CalFresh EBT Card

    You can use your EBT card at most food stores and farmer's markets to buy food.


  4. Reapply after 12 months

    If someone in your household is disabled or elderly, you may be able to wait 24 months before reapplying. Ask your CalFresh caseworker if you think you might qualify.
