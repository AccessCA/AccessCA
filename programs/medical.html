MediCal

Get insurance to help you stay healthy

How does MediCal work?

Medi-Cal is California's Medicaid program. It provides health insurance to low-income individuals including families with children, seniors, persons with disabilities, foster care, pregnant women, and low income people with specific diseases such as tuberculosis, breast cancer or HIV/AIDS.

You can use your MediCal insurance for doctor visits, hospital care, immunization, pregnancy-related services, nursing home care, dental care, mental health services, and more.

How do I apply for MediCal?


  1. Fill out an application online or mail one in


  2. Choose a plan

    Your county should reach out to you within 45 days with a final decision about your benefits. If you are approved for MediCal, you will receive information in the mail asking you to choose an insurance plan. If you do not choose a plan within 30 days, MediCal will choose a plan for you. The health plans available to you depend on what county you live in.


  3. Start using your insurance

    You can use your MediCal insurance for doctor visits, hospital care, immunization, pregnancy-related services, nursing home care, dental care, mental health services, and more.
